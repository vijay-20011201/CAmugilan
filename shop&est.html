Overview

Various State Governments have enacted the Shops and Establishment Act to regulate employees' work conditions in shops, commercial undertakings, restaurants, etc. All commercial establishments should abide by The Weekly Holiday Act, 1942 enacted by the Central Government, which governs the grant of holidays. However, no specific Central Government Act comprehensively governs work hours, payment of wages, health, and safety in commercial establishments. To bridge this gap, state Governments have enacted a Shops and Establishment Act to help regulate the conduct of retail establishments within their jurisdiction. To know more, read the article on "Shops and Establishment Act in India."

Karnataka Shops and Establishment Act

The Karnataka Shops and Establishment Act regulate the operations of shops and commercial establishments. It was introduced to regulate work hours, annual leave with wages, wages, and compensation, employment of women and children, and other aspects of shops or commercial establishments.

Who is Eligible to Register Under This Act?

Every Shop or Establishment who is carrying trade, business, or services within the notified areas in the State of Karnataka should register his establishment under this Act within 30 days from starting the business.
