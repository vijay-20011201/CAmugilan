Frequently Asked Questions

Who needs to file an Income Tax Return (ITR)?
Any individual, HUF, firm, company, or any other person whose gross total income exceeds the basic exemption limit is required to file an ITR. Additionally, persons with foreign assets, wanting refunds, or seeking to carry forward losses must also file.
What is the due date for filing ITR?
For individuals and non-audit cases, the due date is usually July 31st. For businesses requiring audit, it is October 31st. Transfer pricing cases have a deadline of November 30th.
When is a Tax Audit mandatory?
Tax audit under Section 63 of Income Tax Act 2025 is mandatory if business turnover exceeds the prescribed limit or if profession receipts exceed the specified threshold. The audit report must be filed in Form 3CA/3CB with Form 3CD.
What are the penalties for late TDS filing?
Late filing of TDS returns attracts a late fee of ₹200 per day until the return is filed, subject to a maximum of the TDS amount. Additionally, interest @1.5% per month is charged for late deposit of TDS.
What documents are needed for ITR filing?
Common documents include PAN, Aadhaar, Form 16/16A, bank statements, investment proofs (80C, 80D, etc.), capital gains statements, rental income details, and Form 26AS/AIS. Business owners also need P&L and Balance Sheet.
