Expert assistance in obtaining Lower/Nil TDS certificates under Sections 195 & 197 for residents and non-residents.

Lower TDS Deduction Certificate

Indian Income Tax Act provides withholding of tax (TDS) on various payments like rent, professional fees, lottery winnings, interest, property purchase consideration, etc. Tax withholding provision is prevalent in most countries having taxation on income and is based on the principle that tax is paid when it is earned rather than when assessed. It provides cash flow to the government throughout the year.

Income Tax Act provides for tax deduction at a specified rate on gross payment without considering whether the receiver has actual taxable income. If the deductee has lower taxable income or has loss and hence the tax deducted is in excess, they have two options:

  • To claim the excess TDS as a refund when filing a return of income, or
  • Apply for a Lower TDS Deduction Certificate, which allows the deductor to deduct TDS at a rate lower than the prescribed rate or NIL rate.

Lower TDS Certificate for Domestic Deductees

In the case of payments to resident payees, TDS provisions under sections 192 to 194N are applicable. Different rates of deduction and separate thresholds are provided for various payments.

A Lower TDS certificate is issued specific to a deductor with a limit up to which it is applicable. Hence, it is deductor-specific, not a blanket certificate applicable to any deductor.

Lower TDS Certificate for Non-Resident Deductees

Section 195 of the Income Tax Act applies for withholding taxes from payments to Non-Residents. Unlike resident payees, there is no threshold limit below which TDS is not applicable for payments to non-residents.

The provisions of the Double Taxation Avoidance Agreement (DTAA) with the country where the non-resident is resident will determine the applicable tax rate. The non-resident receiver can apply for a lower TDS deduction certificate, allowing the deductor to deduct TDS at the reduced rate mentioned in the certificate.

Lower TDS Deduction Certificate for Non-Resident Indians (Property Sale)

Section 195 applies when a resident buyer purchases property from a Non-Resident Indian (NRI). TDS is applicable at 20% + surcharge & cess on the entire sale consideration.

The NRI seller can apply for a lower/nil deduction certificate by submitting:

  • Agreement to sell with the buyer
  • TAN numbers of the buyers
  • Computation of capital gain
  • Supporting documents for cost of acquisition of the property
  • Income Tax returns for the last three years (or affidavit if income was below taxable limit)
  • Evidence of non-resident status (passport with immigration stamps)

Note: Separate applications are required for joint owners. The department aims to process applications within 30 days. Proposed reinvestments for capital gains exemption are generally not considered at the application stage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is required to file TDS return?
TDS return filing is mandatory for the assessees who come under the tax slab as prescribed by the Income Tax Department.
What is TAN? Is it required to file TDS return?
TAN or Tax Deduction and Collection Account Number (10 digit alphanumeric number) has to be obtained by all individuals who are responsible for deducting or collecting tax. It is compulsory to specify TAN in TDS/TCS return (including any e-TDS/TCS return).
What are different types of TDS forms?
Forms 24Q, 26Q, 27Q, 27EQ, 27D
What is TDS justification report?
Justification report is submitted while processing the statement filed by the deductor in a particular quarter of the financial year. It consists of various defaults or errors identified by the Income Tax Department when a TDS deductor files the statement.
How do I deposit TDS to the central government?
  • To pay TDS online, visit the website of the income tax department
  • Select the Relevant Challan.
  • Fill in the Challan Details.
  • Confirm Challan Details.
  • Make TDS Payment.
  • Online Verification.
Who is required to issue a TDS Certificate?
Every person deducting tax at source is required as per Section 203 to furnish a certificate to the payee that tax has been deducted along with certain other particulars.
