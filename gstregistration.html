GST Registration

Goods and Service Tax is a tax levied on the user's supply of goods and services. GST is a simplified indirect tax structure. The tax paid on goods and services procured by one person can be availed as credit while passing on the goods and services to the next stage until they reach the final consumer who bears tax incidence.

In the GST Regime businesses, if a turnover from the sale of goods exceeds Rs. Forty lakhs (Rs 20 lakhs for North Eastern and hilly states) or turnover from sale of services exceeds Rs 20 lakhs; it is required to register as an average taxable person. For other businesses, registration under GST is mandatory irrespective of turnover, like those paying tax under the reverse charge mechanism, supply to SEZ, Exports, etc.

Who is required to register for GST?
  • Individuals registered under the Pre-GST law (i.e., Excise, VAT, Service Tax, etc.)
  • Businesses with turnover above the threshold limit of Rs. 20 Lakhs (in case of services)/ Rs. 40 Lakhs (in case of products)
  • Businesses with turnover above the threshold limit of Rs. 10 Lakhs (in case of services)/ Rs. 20 Lakhs (in case of products) (for North-Eastern States, J&K, Himachal Pradesh, and Uttarakhand)
  • Agents of a supplier & Input service distributor
  • Those paying tax under the reverse charge mechanism
  • A person who supplies via an e-commerce aggregator
  • Every e-commerce aggregator
  • Person supplying online information and database access or retrieval services from a place outside India to a person in India, other than a registered taxable person
How is GST calculated?
A business whose turnover exceeds Rs 20 lakh in a financial year is required to register under GST. A composition scheme has been introduced under GST for small companies operating in India.
