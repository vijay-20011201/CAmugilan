Frequently Asked Questions

Who needs to register for GST?
Generally, businesses must register for GST if their annual turnover exceeds ₹40 lakhs for the exclusive supply of goods or ₹20 lakhs for services and mixed supplies.(Varies to some states). Section 24 Compulsory Registration & Section 23 Exemption from registration need to be checked case specific
What are the different types of GST returns?
Key returns include GSTR-1 (outward supplies), GSTR-3B (monthly summary), GSTR-9 (annual return), and GSTR-9C (reconciliation statement). Composition dealers file GSTR-4 annually and CMP-08 quarterly.
What is ITC and how does reconciliation work?
Input Tax Credit (ITC) is the GST paid on purchases that can be offset against output tax. Reconciliation involves matching your purchase records with GSTR-2A/2B data to ensure you claim only eligible credits and avoid mismatches that trigger notices.
What is the Composition Scheme?
The Composition Scheme under Section 10(1) & 10(2A) of the CGST Act, 2017, simplifies GST compliance for small taxpayers. Eligible businesses pay tax at a fixed percentage of turnover instead of regular rates. Key features include quarterly payments (Form CMP-08) and an annual return (Form GSTR-4). However, taxpayers under this scheme cannot claim Input Tax Credit (ITC), cannot collect tax from their customers, and are generally barred from making inter-State outward supplies.
What is LUT and who needs it?
A Letter of Undertaking (LUT) is a document furnished under Rule 96A of the CGST Rules, 2017, by a registered person to make zero-rated supplies (i.e., exports or supplies to a Special Economic Zone) without the payment of Integrated GST (IGST). It serves as an undertaking to the government that the exporter will fulfill all the requirements of export, failing which they will pay the applicable IGST along with interest.
