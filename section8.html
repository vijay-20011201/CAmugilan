Section 8

Companies with charitable objects are registered under section 8 of the Companies Act, 2013. A person or an association of persons can be registered under this section if:

  1. their object is to promote commerce, art, science, sports, education, research, social welfare, religion charity, protection of environment or any such other object;
  2. their intention is to apply its profits, if any, or other income in promoting its objects; and
  3. They intend to prohibit the payment of any dividend to its members.

One of the distinct features of Section 8 Company limited by guarantee is that it can start the business without infusing any capital at the time of incorporation.

Advantages

  • A Section 8 Company can be formed with two members only, by Company Act 2013.
  • It is registered as a separate legal entity. It has its rights and obligations.
  • Section 8 Company is a limited liability; the members/partners are not affected. They are liable for unpaid shares only.
  • No minimum share capital is required.
  • Section 8 Company is an NGO. The company doesn't depend upon the life of its members. There will be no effect on the firm's continuance.

Documents Required

  • Pan Card of all Directors and Members (Self attested)
  • Identity Proof of all Directors and Members - DL or Voter ID or Passport (Self attested)
  • 1 Passport size photo of all Directors and Members
  • Address Proof of all Directors and Members- Bank Statement/ Electricity Bill/ Telephone Bill/ Mobile Bill (Self attested and not older than 2 months)
  • Proof of Office address (Conveyance/ Lease Deed/ Rent Agreement along with rent receipts/ Tax paid Receipt/ NOC)
  • Copy of Government issued Utility Bill of Office address (Not older than 2 months)

Frequently Asked Questions

What is registered under section 8 of the Companies Act?
A company is referred to as Section 8 Company when it registered as a Non-Profit Organization (NPO), i.e., when it promotes arts, commerce, education, charity, protection of the environment, sports, science, research, social welfare, religion and intends to use its profits (if any) or other income for promoting these objectives.
Why is Sec 8 preferred over NGO or trust?
The main reason why Section 8 is preferred is that it has got several exemptions in terms of tax and others.
Is GST applicable to Section 8 companies?
No GST does not apply to Section 8 Companies.
Is Section 8 company a private company?
Section 8 companies also forms as a private limited or public limited company having limited liability. Members of this company have limited liability as per their share subscribed. This company doesn't issue a dividend to its members because it is restricted from the law.
