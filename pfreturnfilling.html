Frequently Asked Questions

Which establishments are required to register under EPFO?
Any establishment employing 20 or more persons is required to register under the Employees' Provident Fund and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952. Once registered, the establishment must continue PF compliance even if employee strength falls below 20.
What is the PF contribution rate for employers and employees?
Both employer and employee contribute 12% of the employee's basic salary + dearness allowance. Out of the employer's 12%, 8.33% goes to the Pension Fund (subject to a maximum of ₹1,250) and the remaining 3.67% to the PF account. Additionally, employers contribute 0.5% towards EDLI and 0.5% as administrative charges.
What is ECR and how is it filed?
Electronic Challan-cum-Return (ECR) is a monthly return that includes employee-wise details of PF contributions. It must be uploaded on the EPFO portal along with the payment challan by the 15th of every month. ECR includes employee name, UAN, wages, contribution amount, and other details.
What happens if PF returns are filed late?
Late filing or payment attracts damages under Section 14B of the EPF Act at the rate of 12% per annum on the delayed amount. Additionally, administrative charges may be levied. Persistent default can lead to prosecution and imprisonment.
Can employees withdraw their PF before retirement?
Yes, employees can withdraw PF for specific purposes like medical emergency, higher education, home purchase/construction, or marriage. Partial withdrawal is allowed under certain conditions. Full withdrawal is permitted if the employee is unemployed for 2 months or more. Withdrawals before 5 years of service are taxable.
What is UAN and why is it important?
Universal Account Number (UAN) is a unique 12-digit number allotted to each employee. It remains the same throughout the employee's career, even when changing jobs. UAN helps in tracking PF contributions across different employers and facilitates online PF management, including balance checking, claim filing, and passbook download.
What documents are needed for PF registration?
Required documents include PAN card of establishment, incorporation/registration certificate, address proof of establishment, bank account details (cancelled cheque), details of directors/partners, employee list with Aadhaar and bank details, and digital signature of authorized signatory. The registration is done online through the EPFO portal.
Is PF contribution mandatory for all employees?
PF contribution is mandatory for all employees drawing basic salary + DA up to ₹15,000 per month. For employees earning more than ₹15,000, PF contribution is voluntary unless they were already members before crossing this threshold. Contractual, temporary, and part-time employees are also covered if they meet the salary criteria.
