Frequently Asked Questions

What is ESIC and who needs to register?
ESIC (Employee State Insurance Corporation) is a social security scheme providing medical and cash benefits to employees. Any establishment with 10 or more employees (20 or more in some states) earning up to ₹21,000 per month must register under ESIC Act, 1948.
What is the ESIC contribution rate?
The employer contributes 3.25% and the employee contributes 0.75% of the employee's gross wages. The total ESIC contribution is 4% of gross salary, subject to the wage ceiling of ₹21,000 per month.
When is ESIC contribution due?
ESIC contributions must be paid by the 15th of the following month along with the filing of monthly returns. Late payment attracts interest and penalties under the ESI Act.
What documents are required for ESIC registration?
Required documents include PAN card of the establishment, incorporation/registration certificate, address proof, bank account details, list of employees with Aadhaar and salary details, and authorized signatory details. Registration is done online through the ESIC portal.
What benefits do employees get under ESIC?
Employees covered under ESIC receive medical benefits for themselves and their family members, sickness benefit (cash allowance during illness), maternity benefit, disablement benefit, dependents' benefit in case of death, and funeral expenses. Medical treatment is provided at ESIC hospitals and dispensaries free of cost.
How is ESIC number generated for employees?
Once the employer is registered, employee ESIC numbers (IP numbers) are generated through the ESIC portal by uploading employee details including Aadhaar, bank account, and other KYC information. Each employee receives a unique 17-digit Insurance Number.
What are the penalties for non-compliance?
Non-compliance with ESIC regulations can lead to penalties, imprisonment up to 3 years, interest on delayed contributions at 12% per annum, and damages. Failure to register or delayed payment of contributions attracts strict legal action under the ESI Act.
Can employees opt out of ESIC?
No, employees earning up to ₹21,000 per month in covered establishments cannot opt out of ESIC. It is a mandatory social security scheme. However, employees earning above ₹21,000 per month are not covered under ESIC.
