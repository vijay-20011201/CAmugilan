Professional Tax in Karnataka

Profession Tax is collected by the state government and applies to salaried employees and individuals. Employees working in organizations or establishments (like factories) must register for professional tax in Karnataka.

Professional tax registration is mandatory for businesses within 30 days of employing staff. Employers deduct PT from salaries and deposit it with the government. The maximum PT is capped at ₹2,400 per annum.

There are two types of Profession Tax for business entities:

  1. PT for the entity: ₹1,000–₹2,500 per annum (deposited by April 30th each year).
  2. PT deducted from employees: ₹200/month for salaries ≥ ₹15,000 (as per Karnataka PT Act).

Enrollment vs Registration

Certificate of Enrollment (EC)

Under Karnataka Act 35 of 1976, businesses (Sl. No. 2–74 in Schedule) must obtain EC from the Profession Tax Officer. Companies enroll to pay annual tax by April 30th.

Certificate of Registration (RC)

Required for entities (Sole Prop, Partnership, LLP, Companies) with employees earning ≥ ₹15,000/month. Employers deduct and deposit PT.

Documents Required

  • Address proof of establishment & partners/proprietor/directors
  • PAN card of partners/proprietor/directors
  • Photos of partners/proprietor/directors
  • Salary details of employees
  • Financial statements of the establishment
  • For companies: Certificate of Incorporation & address proof

Eligibility & Liability

  • Individuals earning ≥ ₹15,000/month pay ₹200/month (₹2,400/year max)
  • For professionals (legal/medical/consultants), PT depends on years of service

Who Has to Pay Professional Tax

Applies to individuals & entities in professions, trades, or employment (government & private).

Employers deduct from salaried employees; self-employed pay directly.

Business entities liable:

  • Sole Proprietor
  • Partnership Firms
  • Limited Liability Partnership
  • Corporations
  • Hindu Undivided Family (HUF)
  • Societies/Clubs/Associations

Should a private limited company get PT registration?
Every business entity in Karnataka should obtain the Certificate of Enrollment (EC) from the Profession Tax Officer within Thirty days from the date of commencement of their business. The PT registration is mandatory for businesses whether they employ people or not.
