MSME / Udyam Registration

Udyog Aadhaar or MSME registration is given to Micro, Small and Medium-sized Businesses under the MSME Development Act, 2006. Classification is based on investment in plant & machinery/equipment and annual turnover.

Classification Micro Small Medium
Manufacturing & Service Enterprises Investment ≤ ₹1 crore

Benefits of MSME Registration

  • 1% exemption on interest rate for Overdraft
  • Collateral-free loans at 2% lower interest
  • Reimbursement of ISO certification expenses
  • Preference in government tenders
  • Easier licenses, approvals & registrations
  • 15% CLCSS subsidy on automated machinery
  • Eligibility for IPS subsidies
  • Exemption in stamp duty
  • 15-year exemption on electricity duty
  • 50% subsidy on patent registration
  • Capital investment subsidy
  • Consideration for international trade fairs

Eligibility for MSME Registration

Micro Enterprises

  • Manufacturing: Investment < ₹25 lakh in plant & machinery
  • Service: Investment < ₹10 lakh in equipment

Small Enterprises

  • Manufacturing: Investment ₹25 lakh – ₹5 crore
  • Service: Investment ₹10 lakh – ₹2 crore

Medium Enterprises

  • Manufacturing: Investment ₹5 crore – ₹10 crore
  • Service: Investment ₹2 crore – ₹5 crore

Note: Land & building not counted. Original cost considered (no depreciation). Any entity (proprietorship, partnership, company, LLP) can apply.

Documents Required

  • Aadhaar Card & PAN Card of proprietor/applicant/authorised signatory (mandatory)
  • For Partnership: Aadhaar & PAN of applicant partner
  • For Company/LLP: Aadhaar & PAN of authorised signatory
  • If no Aadhaar: Apply at enrolment centre first

Frequently Asked Questions

How can you use Udyam registration in business?
Udyam Registration or MSME Registration - a government registration provided along with a recognition certificate and a unique number. This is to certify small/medium businesses or enterprises
Which startup comes under the MSME?

A startup will be classified as a micro, small or medium enterprise based on the following criteria:

Micro

When the investment in plant & machinery or equipment does not exceed Rupees One Crore & the turnover doesn't exceed Rupees five crores.

Small

When the investment in plant & machinery or equipment does not exceed Rupees Ten Crores and the turnover doesn't exceed Rupees Fifty Crores.

Medium

When the investment in plant & machinery or equipment does not exceed Rupees Fifty Crores and the turnover doesn't exceed Rupees two hundred & fifty crores

Do restaurants and hotels come under MSME?
At the 40th meeting of Small Scale Industries, the Government of India has decided that hotels, tourism, construction of Cinema Houses, tea processing and blending, diagnostic centers etc can come under MSME.
Do vegetable sellers and street vendors come under MSME's?
Yes. PM Street Vendors's AtmaNirbhar Nidhi (PM SVANidhi) is the scheme for the street vendors
